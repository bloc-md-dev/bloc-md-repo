Asian American History
[Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882] banned Chinese immigration and intensified anti-Chinese violence. In 1877, an [[Anti-Chinese Riot]] burned down San Jose. Chinese Americans also built [[Mutual Aid Societies]] called [[huiguan]] (district associations) and [[tongs]] (fraternal organizations). [[Queue Ordinance of 1876]] also discriminated against Chinese immigrants.

[[Filipino Community Center]] in San Jose directly aided the [[Delano Grape Strike]]. 

Other potential leaders could be [[Felomina Tirona]]. She was a Filipina labor organizer in the 1950s and 1960s. She mobilized workers in San Jose. Another potential person could be [[Mary Acosta]]. She was a Latina and Filipina farmer who advocated for bilingual labor rights and education.
